Introduction

In the beginning of Abh history there were 29 individual Abh who lived and worked together on the same colony ship, the Ablïarsec. Each one of these crew members had a different responsibility, and inevitably passed that responsibility on to the next generation, and clans were formed. As population grew, clans began to be comprised of multiple families. Originally every clan had a leader who had some say in matters such as where the ship would go, and the highest leader was the king of the Ablïarsec clan. This clan was well suited to lead and its' members had leadership qualities not found in some other clans. When the empire was formed, eight royal families existed within the Ablïarsec clan, and the highest ranking member in the Star Forces in that clan was the Emperor, or Empress. Each of the eight royal families was assigned with the task of governing one of the eight kingdoms that made up the empire. While other clan's maintained their kings and queens, such titles became honorary, and no longer carried any real authority.

This article details the 29 original clans of the Abh empire. The Ablïarsec clan has no astrological significance, however the other 28 are all named after constellations named by the Japanese ancestors of the Abh. Those 28 constellations can be seen in the map below. Additionally, almost every clan has a distinctive physical trait that only members of that clan have. These traits are maintained through careful genetic manipulation. Clan listings are divided up by the order they appear in the constellation map.

New Clans Argument

While it is an established part of series canon that new families are constantly being started, it is never directly stated or implied that new clans can be started, however after careful consideration of all the available data, one must come to the logical conclusion that new clans in addition to the original 29 do exist. By carefully reviewing the names of the Abh who appear in the series, one notices that many of the Abh belong to clans not mentioned in the Reader's Guide list of the 29 original clans. Some examples of such names include Laicch, Linn, Atosoryac, Samsonn, Sobach, Aicryac, Cfadiss, and Cénéch, just to name a few. Therefore one must logically conclude that at present, more than 29 clans exist.
